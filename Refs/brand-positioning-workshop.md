# Breathe Brand Positioning

Last updated: 2026-04-18
Method: Lunour positioning and messaging sprint
Status: working direction

## Goal

Lock Pillar 1 and Pillar 2 before pushing visual identity further.

This document is the simplified output of the positioning workshop.
It keeps only the direction we want to build on.

## Brand architecture

- Company name: `Breathe`
- Product name: `Breathe`
- `Luminous Ops`: visual territory
- Voice in the brand: signature capability, not category

## Positioning summary

### Primary ICP

- in-house high-volume hiring teams
- frontline and operational hiring environments
- recurring or spiky hiring across many open roles
- likely buyers: talent acquisition leadership, recruiting operations, hiring leadership

### Secondary ICP

- staffing firms and staffing operators
- labor-intensive, multi-role recruiting environments
- useful as a dedicated landing later, not as the primary homepage audience

### Main enemy

Recruiting teams overwhelmed by applicant volume, spread across multiple tools, and forced to push candidates manually through an inconsistent process.

This is more important than any single named competitor.

### Competitive context

Competitors to track:

- `Alex.ai`
- `Orbio`
- `Clara.works`
- `Maki People` as a more white-collar reference point

Strategic stance:

- Breathe should not sound like an AI recruiter
- Breathe should not sound like an ATS replacement
- Breathe should not sound like a white-collar assessment platform

## Category

`AI hiring engine for high-volume teams`

Why this works:

- `AI` explains why the workflow can now move differently
- `engine` suggests execution and leverage without implying full ATS replacement
- `high-volume teams` keeps the category operational and specific

## Positioning statement

For in-house high-volume hiring teams that struggle to fill roles fast enough, Breathe is the AI hiring engine that automates the manual work between application and hire across the full hiring flow, so teams can fill more roles, faster on top of the ATS they already use.

## One-liner

`Automate the manual work between application and hire.`

## Core promise

`Fill more roles, faster.`

## Mechanism

Breathe uses AI to automate the manual work that usually slows hiring down:

- screening
- outreach and follow-up
- interviews
- candidate movement through the workflow
- recruiter input only where judgment or approval is needed

## Key messaging hierarchy

1. Automate the manual work between application and hire.
2. Fill more roles, faster.
3. The workflow moves itself.
4. Recruiters focus on decisions, not admin.
5. Work on top of the ATS teams already use.

## Elevator pitch

Breathe helps in-house high-volume hiring teams fill more roles by taking over the manual work that usually sits between application and hire. Using AI, it screens candidates, drives outreach and follow-up, runs interviews, moves candidates through the workflow, and asks recruiters for input only where judgment is needed. It works on top of the ATS teams already use, so they can automate more of the hiring flow without replacing their stack.

## Hero direction

### Headline

`Automate the manual work between application and hire.`

### Subheadline

`Breathe helps high-volume teams fill more roles, faster by automating screening, outreach, interviews, and coordination, so recruiters focus on decisions, not admin.`

## Proof direction

Strongest proof currently available:

- `From the team that increased recruiter productivity 7x at Job&Talent.`

Additional proof areas to develop:

- more candidate coverage
- faster movement through the funnel
- recruiter productivity gains
- ability to automate more of the workflow on top of the ATS

## Voice attributes

### Direct

Is:

- clear
- outcome-led
- low-jargon

Is not:

- vague
- fluffy
- robotic

### Confident

Is:

- specific
- technically credible
- decisive

Is not:

- inflated
- salesy swagger
- anthropomorphic AI hype

### Operational

Is:

- grounded in throughput, workflow, and execution
- close to how high-volume teams actually work

Is not:

- generic innovation language
- HR-tech softness
- abstract AI theater

### Precise

Is:

- European in tone
- controlled
- deliberate

Is not:

- loud
- overly Americanized
- casual for the sake of friendliness

## Guardrails

Avoid:

- `AI recruiter`
- `voice-powered hiring` as top-level positioning
- anything that implies Breathe replaces the ATS
- anything that frames the product as a narrow interview tool

Keep:

- industrial hardness in the visual language
- precision in the copy
- AI as mechanism, not as hype
- workflow automation as the core truth
