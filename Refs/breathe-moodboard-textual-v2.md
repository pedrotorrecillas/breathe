# Breathe Moodboard Textual v2

Last updated: 2026-04-19
Method: Lunour visual territory refinement
Status: proposal

## Goal

Sharpen the Breathe visual territory so it feels:

- operational
- high-volume
- precise
- unmistakably technological
- clearly AI-enabled

without collapsing into:

- chatbot recruiter aesthetics
- generic AI futurism
- ATS replacement visuals
- white-collar assessment branding

## Core visual thesis

`Breathe should feel like AI quietly operating real-world hiring flow.`

The visual job is not just to show work.
It is to show work being carried forward by an intelligent system.

That means the brand needs both:

- physical operational realism
- visible computational intelligence

Not one without the other.

## What we have so far

Strong existing ingredients:

- operations in motion
- industrial realism
- systems over portraits
- luminous surfaces with hard black contrast
- subtle signal overlays
- structured, assertive UI

Current weakness:

- we are better at showing operations than showing intelligence
- if we are not careful, the brand could look like logistics software, industrial ops software, or a general operations consultancy

## New recommendation

Keep the operational base, but increase the visible sense of AI through:

### 1. Computational overlays that feel functional

Use:

- candidate-routing paths
- status transitions
- logic markers
- ranking or prioritization cues
- decision checkpoints
- machine-readable grids
- queue-state labels
- low-profile data trails

These should suggest:

- the system is interpreting
- the system is deciding what moves next
- the system is coordinating the flow

Not just:

- decoration
- abstract "tech energy"

### 2. Interfaces that feel algorithmic, not dashboard-generic

Product and supporting UI should include:

- state changes
- workflow progression
- handoff logic
- ranked queues
- automation states
- human review checkpoints

The UI should feel like:

- work is being processed
- tasks are being resolved
- candidates are being advanced

The UI should not feel like:

- static analytics
- BI reporting
- CRM cards only

### 3. Imagery where intelligence is visible in the environment

Images should not only show physical workplaces.
They should feel subtly instrumented.

Examples:

- warehouse aisle with faint routing paths layered over movement
- production zone with status markers tied to motion
- candidate flow represented through repeated structural elements
- operational surfaces with visible scanning, sequencing, or progression logic

The image should imply:

`the system is reading the workflow`

### 4. Motion that feels like autonomous progression

Motion should communicate:

- the workflow advancing
- things being queued and released
- signals activating next steps
- candidates moving through stages without manual pushing

Avoid:

- playful bounce
- abstract glowing particles
- “AI magic” swirls

Use:

- directional wipe
- stage-to-stage reveal
- pulse on progression
- low-profile path animation
- density change as flow signal

## AI cues Breathe can own

These are better than generic AI tropes because they connect directly to product truth.

### Candidate flow intelligence

Visualize:

- movement from application to next step
- automated transitions
- prioritization
- branching logic

Why it works:

- specific to workflow automation
- helps explain why AI matters
- avoids assistant-persona territory

### Human-in-the-loop checkpoints

Visualize:

- moments where the system pauses for recruiter approval
- review gates
- flagged decisions
- selective escalation

Why it works:

- communicates controlled AI
- differentiates from black-box automation
- reinforces trust

### Signal embedded in operations

Visualize:

- low-key overlays attached to real objects, motion, or queues
- not floating randomly
- not holographic UI

Why it works:

- makes AI visible without making it fake

## Hero image guidance

The hero should show:

- a real operational environment
- visible motion or throughput
- subtle signs of computational flow
- enough structure to imply system logic

The hero should not show:

- a chatbot conversation as the primary image
- a smiling recruiter at a laptop
- a fake futuristic interface floating in space
- a literal robot / avatar / assistant figure

## Inclusion rules

Prioritize images and references with:

- repeated structures
- queues
- movement
- machine rhythm
- controlled blur
- visible sequencing
- status-like cues
- instrumented environments
- panelized UI with workflow states

## Exclusion rules

Reject images and references with:

- HR stock photography
- office collaboration scenes
- generic SaaS gradients
- holographic AI graphics
- over-branded abstract 3D shapes
- purely analytical dashboards
- overly humanized assistant visuals
- heavy psychometric / data-science aesthetics

## Competitor separation

### Against Clara

Avoid:

- AI recruiter persona
- interview-first visuals
- over-humanized AI

Prefer:

- system movement
- workflow logic
- operational automation

### Against Paradox

Avoid:

- chat-first design
- assistant feel
- candidate-conversation-centric imagery

Prefer:

- harder system surfaces
- operational cues
- execution over conversation

### Against Maki

Avoid:

- assessment-science feel
- psychometric rigor aesthetics
- enterprise-intelligence theater

Prefer:

- throughput
- progression
- real environments

### Against Orbio

Avoid:

- full-suite HR system feel
- too much platform grandeur

Prefer:

- precise hiring focus
- AI layer on top of the ATS

## Visual formula

If we had to reduce the whole moodboard to one formula:

`Operational environment + visible throughput + embedded computational logic + hard editorial system`

## Suggested next references to seek

Look for references that combine:

- industrial photography
- motion blur
- transport / workflow sequencing
- machine-readable visual systems
- low-profile overlays
- editorial technology layouts

The best references will make you feel:

- the work is moving
- the system is thinking
- the humans are still in control
