# Breathe Brand Process

Last updated: 2026-04-17
Method: Lunour four-pillar process
Status: active working plan

## Objective

Build the `Breathe` brand as a strategic system, not just a visual identity.

This means we will work in order:

1. positioning
2. messaging
3. creative direction
4. brand system

## Current working assumptions

- `Breathe` is the market-facing brand
- `Luminous Ops` is a visual and strategic territory, not the public name
- the category is currently `AI hiring infrastructure for high-volume teams`
- voice is a signature capability, not the whole category

These are working assumptions, not final truth.

## Phase 1: Discovery

Goal:
understand what we are really building, for whom, and against what alternatives

Outputs:

- brand architecture decision
- competitive alternatives list
- competitor map with direct and adjacent players
- primary and secondary ICP
- trigger events
- list of strongest proof points we already have

Working questions:

- who buys this first?
- what are they replacing?
- what pain gets budget approved?
- what language do they already use internally?
- which competitors are closest in product claim?
- which competitors are closest in visual language?

## Phase 2: Strategy

Goal:
lock Pillar 1 and Pillar 2

Outputs:

- one market category
- one positioning statement
- one one-liner
- one elevator pitch
- 3 to 5 core messages
- voice attributes with is/isn't rules

Current draft direction:

- category: `AI hiring infrastructure for high-volume teams`
- one-liner: `Turn applicant volume into qualified hiring flow.`
- promise: `Move high-volume hiring with speed, signal and control.`

## Phase 3: Creative Direction

Goal:
translate strategy into an identity direction

Outputs:

- 2 to 3 creative territories
- typography direction
- color system direction
- photography and graphic treatment rules
- homepage concept exploration

Current recommendation:

- keep the strongest parts of `Luminous Ops`
- make sure every visual choice supports the strategy
- avoid treating mood as a substitute for clarity

## Phase 4: Brand System

Goal:
make the brand usable by a team, not just discussable in a doc

Outputs:

- quick-reference brand card
- messaging source of truth
- landing page rules
- basic UI/system tokens
- first templates for deck and marketing surfaces

## What we should do next

The next practical move is to finish Phase 1 and Phase 2 in writing.

That means resolving:

1. primary ICP
2. main alternative we replace
3. strongest proof claims
4. final category choice
5. homepage message hierarchy
