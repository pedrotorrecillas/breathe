# Breathe Creative Direction Brief

Last updated: 2026-04-18
Method: Lunour creative direction phase
Status: working brief

## Goal

Translate the current Breathe strategy into a visual direction for landing, identity, and product marketing surfaces.

This brief starts from the strongest ideas in `Luminous Ops`, but updates them to match the positioning we have now locked:

- `Breathe` is the public brand
- `Luminous Ops` is a visual territory, not the public name
- Breathe is an `AI hiring engine for high-volume teams`
- the brand should not feel like an AI recruiter or an ATS replacement

## Strategic foundation

### One-liner

`Automate the manual work between application and hire.`

### Core promise

`Fill more roles, faster.`

### Supporting truths

- the workflow moves itself
- recruiters focus on decisions, not admin
- Breathe works on top of the ATS teams already use

### What the design needs to communicate

- this is an operational system, not a chatbot
- this is execution infrastructure, not HR fluff
- this is AI applied to workflow movement, not generic AI theater
- this is high-volume-first, not white-collar-first

## What we keep from Luminous Ops

These ideas still feel strong and aligned:

- operations in motion
- industrial realism
- systems over portraiture
- hard black contrast on a luminous base
- signal accents used sparingly
- editorial, assertive typography
- visible structure, grids, and panels
- conversion-first product marketing

## What we change from Luminous Ops

These points need adjustment:

- `voice` should no longer behave like a top-level brand story
- `speed, signal and control` is useful internally, but too abstract as hero messaging
- `AI operating system` is too close to ATS/system replacement language
- `American dynamism` should soften into a more European and precise tone

Updated read:

- keep the hardness
- reduce the swagger
- keep the technical confidence
- increase precision and clarity

## Creative territory

### Core direction

`Operational intelligence in motion.`

This is the visual translation of:

- manual work becoming automated flow
- candidate throughput becoming visible system movement
- AI operating behind the scenes as execution logic

The system should feel like it is carrying work forward.

## Visual principles

### 1. Operations over people

The subject is the workflow, not the smiling candidate or recruiter.

Use:

- movement
- repetition
- queues
- structured environments
- industrial spaces
- throughput cues

Avoid:

- hero portraits
- lifestyle office photography
- generic HR stock
- over-personalized candidate imagery

### 2. Intelligence embedded in real work

AI should feel integrated into ongoing operations.

Use:

- routing lines
- subtle tracking markers
- density maps
- low-profile pulse cues
- technical overlays that feel functional

Avoid:

- sci-fi futures
- neon cyberpunk
- hologram tropes
- decorative "AI" effects

### 3. Hard structure, luminous contrast

The interface should feel built, not decorated.

Use:

- strong type
- visible borders
- compact components
- disciplined spacing
- black or graphite anchors
- light surfaces with signal accents

Avoid:

- soft SaaS pills everywhere
- floating glass cards
- pastel friendliness
- generic premium dashboard styling

### 4. Precision over personality theater

The brand should feel controlled and exact.

Use:

- sharp language
- measured composition
- explicit hierarchy
- clear product explanation early

Avoid:

- anthropomorphic AI language
- playful assistant metaphors
- exaggerated futurist posing

## Recommended visual blend

The strongest direction is still a blend of the original Luminous concepts, but with different emphasis:

### 1. Blurred Throughput

Keep:

- motion blur
- physical movement
- visible pace and pressure

Why:

This best represents high-volume hiring as throughput rather than as conversation.

### 2. Signal Over Work

Keep:

- subtle AI and routing overlays
- embedded intelligence
- operational signal in physical environments

Why:

This helps explain the AI layer without turning the brand into abstract tech.

### 3. Hard Light Systems

Keep:

- strong type
- machine-like layout
- industrial hardness
- visible structural rhythm

Why:

This prevents the brand from collapsing into generic HR SaaS.

## Color direction

Use the Luminous palette as a base, with slight reinterpretation:

- `Canvas` `#F3F2EC`
- `Ash` `#E5E3DA`
- `Graphite` `#111111`
- `Iron` `#2A2A2A`
- `Signal Lime` `#D9FF57`
- `Cold Blue` `#A8C7FF`

Optional use only:

- `Voice Teal` `#7AF0D2`

Guidance:

- light backgrounds
- dark type
- lime and blue used as signal, not decoration
- teal only where the product genuinely references audio or live interaction

## Typography direction

### Headline type

Should feel:

- assertive
- engineered
- slightly editorial
- compact and hard-edged

### Body type

Should feel:

- clear
- utilitarian
- readable at dense settings

### Supporting accents

Use tabular or mono styles for:

- metrics
- status labels
- workflow markers
- timestamps
- system cues

## Photography and imagery

### Environments to favor

- warehouse aisles
- fulfillment spaces
- loading areas
- industrial prep and packaging
- contact-center density
- repetitive operational work zones

### Framing

- side and rear angles
- partial crops
- motion blur
- system-led compositions
- anonymous or secondary human presence

### Purpose

The image should make the viewer feel:

- volume
- process
- movement
- operational pressure

Not:

- empathy-first storytelling
- workplace culture
- polished recruitment lifestyle

## Layout direction

### Page feel

- conversion-first
- product marketing first, mood second
- bold hierarchy early
- explanation visible above the fold
- rhythm built from contrast between image and system panels

### Structure

Use:

- asymmetry where useful
- panel logic
- section shifts in pace
- strong headline blocks
- explicit CTA placement

Avoid:

- default startup landing symmetry
- empty whitespace posing as luxury
- interchangeable card-grid marketing

## UI adaptation notes

`shadcn/ui` should be used as a base, not as a style.

### Change aggressively

- radii
- borders
- type scale
- density
- panel styling
- navigation feel
- motion behavior

### Keep

- accessibility
- composability
- semantic structure

### Target feel

- control surface
- operations dashboard meets editorial site
- product confidence without enterprise bloat

## Homepage direction

### Hero message

- Headline: `Automate the manual work between application and hire.`
- Subheadline: `Breathe helps high-volume teams fill more roles, faster by automating screening, outreach, interviews, and coordination, so recruiters focus on decisions, not admin.`

### What the hero must show

- movement across the workflow
- AI as invisible engine
- ATS-compatible layer, not replacement system
- enough operational context to feel real

### What the hero must not imply

- chatbot recruiter
- assistant persona
- interview-only product
- broad HR suite

## Visual guardrails

Avoid:

- soft HR-tech aesthetics
- assistant-avatar motifs
- cute chat bubbles as core device
- glossy AI futurism
- over-explaining voice visually

Keep:

- industrial hardness
- luminous surfaces
- crisp product explanation
- workflow movement
- operational realism
