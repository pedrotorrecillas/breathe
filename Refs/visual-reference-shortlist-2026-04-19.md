# Breathe Visual Reference Shortlist

Last updated: 2026-04-19
Method: Lunour visual reference selection
Status: working shortlist

## Goal

Rebuild the visual reference shortlist with `Palantir` as the tonal north star.

This shortlist is not about copying another company.
It is about identifying the strongest ingredients for Breathe's visual identity.

## North star

### 1. Palantir AIP / Foundry

Why it matters:

- serious technology posture
- intelligence connected to operations
- system-level gravity
- control-surface feeling
- software that feels mission-critical

What to steal:

- the sense that the system is doing consequential work
- operational AI instead of AI theater
- density without clutter
- precision in language and interface framing
- the connection between AI and real workflows

What not to copy:

- defense / surveillance associations
- war-room darkness
- institutional coldness
- security-state aesthetics

How Breathe should reinterpret it:

- lighter surfaces
- more visible flow and movement
- more frontline operational context
- less command center, more workflow progression

## Supporting references

### 2. Procore by Instrument

Why it matters:

- construction-grade realism
- bold, structured system
- operational credibility
- industrial environment translated into a modern brand system

What to steal:

- sturdy typography
- rigid systems with texture
- respect for real operational environments
- brand system that feels built for actual work

What not to copy:

- construction-specific cues
- earthy heaviness if it slows the brand down too much

Role in the system:

operational realism and industrial trust

### 3. Fyxer by Red Antler

Why it matters:

- strong example of invisible intelligence made tangible
- the system works quietly in the background
- helpful reference for AI that should not become a mascot

What to steal:

- subtle ways to visualize intelligence
- support-system feeling without anthropomorphism
- intelligence that works behind the scenes

What not to copy:

- any softness that makes the product feel too assistant-like

Role in the system:

quiet AI layer and invisible intelligence

### 4. Ramp by Red Antler

Why it matters:

- B2B strength
- sharp hierarchy
- forceful but controlled contrast
- confidence without too much decoration

What to steal:

- large-scale typography
- strong black anchors
- color as signal
- crisp product-marketing confidence

What not to copy:

- fintech luxury cues
- corporate-card posture that feels too executive rather than operational

Role in the system:

headline energy and visual authority

### 5. Lovable by Primary

Why it matters:

- kinetic system behavior
- motion as product energy
- technology that feels alive

What to steal:

- pulsing system logic
- movement cues
- sense of momentum

What not to copy:

- startup playfulness
- over-lively AI energy
- anything that makes Breathe feel whimsical

Role in the system:

motion and energy reference only

## Working formula

If we combine the shortlist correctly, Breathe should feel like:

- `Palantir` for system seriousness
- `Procore` for operational realism
- `Fyxer` for invisible intelligence
- `Ramp` for visual authority
- `Lovable` for controlled motion

## Point 3 status: what we have in Pillar 3

## What is already solid

These are now clear enough to guide real visual work:

- Breathe should feel like a technology company, not just an operations brand
- the visual system should show AI as operational intelligence, not as a chatbot
- the environment should be frontline and high-volume, not office-centric
- the brand should feel hard, precise, and structured
- the product should look like a layer on top of the ATS, not a replacement suite
- the system should suggest that the workflow moves itself

## What is directionally strong but not fully locked

- the exact ratio between photography and UI
- how visible the AI overlays should be
- how much motion blur is too much
- how “Palantir-like” the brand should feel before it becomes too cold
- whether the hero should be more environmental or more system-led

## What is still open

- exact typography choices
- exact motion rules
- exact visual motifs that become signature assets
- final color ratios
- homepage section-by-section art direction
- final image rules by placement

## Recommendation

Pillar 3 is now strong enough to move into:

1. a reference board with real screenshots and image examples
2. a homepage section architecture
3. early hero explorations

It is not ready yet for:

- final identity lock
- final logo decisions
- polished mockups treated as final truth

## Reference use rules

Use Palantir as:

- tone reference
- system gravity reference
- AI + operations reference

Do not use Palantir as:

- color reference
- industry reference
- literal UI style reference

Use the others to keep Breathe from becoming too dark, too institutional, or too abstract.
