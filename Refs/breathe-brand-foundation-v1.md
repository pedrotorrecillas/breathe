# Breathe Brand Foundation

Last updated: 2026-04-20

## Overview

### What Breathe is

`Breathe` is an `AI hiring engine for high-volume teams`.

Its ownable territory is:

`candidate operations`

It should be understood as:

- a candidate operations layer
- operating on top of the ATS teams already use
- covering the flow from application through documentation and enrollment
- built for high-volume frontline and operational hiring

It should not be understood as:

- an AI recruiter
- an ATS replacement
- a conversational assistant
- a white-collar assessment platform

### Brand architecture

- company name: `Breathe`
- product name: `Breathe`
- `Luminous Ops`: visual territory only

### Primary audience

- in-house high-volume hiring teams
- frontline and operational environments
- recurring or spiky hiring across many open roles
- likely buyers: TA leadership, recruiting operations, hiring leadership

### Secondary audience

- staffing firms
- relevant, but should have their own path later

### Main enemy

Recruiting teams overwhelmed by applicant volume, spread across multiple tools, and forced to push candidates manually through an inconsistent process.

### Category

`AI hiring engine for high-volume teams`

### Ownable territory

`Candidate operations for high-volume teams`

### Positioning statement

For in-house high-volume hiring teams that struggle to fill roles fast enough, Breathe is the AI hiring engine that runs candidate operations from application to ready-to-start, so teams can fill more roles, faster on top of the ATS and systems they already use.

## Visual Foundation

### Core visual thesis

Breathe should feel like AI quietly operating real-world hiring flow.

### Visual formula

`Palantir seriousness + Sierra composure + Luminous throughput + Breathe hiring specificity`

### What the design must communicate

- this is a technology company
- this is an operational system, not a chatbot
- this is AI applied to workflow movement
- this is high-volume-first
- this is not an ATS replacement

### Visual ingredients

- operational environments
- visible throughput and movement
- computational overlays that feel functional
- hard black contrast on luminous surfaces
- structured panels and editorial typography
- workflow and queue cues

### What to avoid

- HR SaaS softness
- assistant-avatar motifs
- defense / surveillance aesthetics
- white-collar assessment science
- generic AI futurism
- interview-only product framing

### Typography

- headline: `Archivo Black`
- body: `IBM Plex Sans`
- mono labels: `IBM Plex Mono`

### Color system

- background: `#F2EDE3`
- surface: `#E6DED0`
- primary text / structure: `#171717`
- secondary structure: `#323232`
- accent: `#5E7EFF`
- accent soft: `#AAB8E8`
- muted lines / borders: `#C8BFAF`

Recommended ratio:

- 72% warm bone and stone neutrals
- 22% soot / graphite structure
- 6% blue accents

### Image system

The image system should stop the brand from collapsing into abstract control-room software.

The image formula should be:

- operational realism
- workflow movement
- restrained system overlays

Landing image style:

- premium editorial technology image-making
- calm, expensive, and precise rather than loud or futuristic
- real operational environments treated as branded surfaces
- overlays embedded into the scene, not floating above it
- subtle oxide-blue system cues on warm bone and stone foundations
- documentary-first photography is preferred over synthetic UI-heavy compositions

Imagery should:

- prove that Breathe belongs in real frontline hiring environments
- make throughput visible and felt
- keep the brand from drifting into defense-tech abstraction
- add pressure, repetition, motion, and physicality

The workflow remains the protagonist.
Humans appear as part of the system, not as the emotional center of the brand.

#### Inclusion rules

Use imagery that shows:

- warehouse aisles
- loading and staging areas
- prep surfaces
- contact-center density
- repeated structures
- movement, queues, handoffs, or flow
- physical environments where high-volume hiring is believable

Look for frames that communicate:

- repetition
- throughput
- operational pressure
- progression
- systemized work

#### Exclusion rules

Do not use:

- smiling recruiter portraits
- office teamwork scenes
- people looking at laptops
- stock-photo “happy team” imagery
- generic abstract 3D AI backgrounds
- control-room imagery without any operational grounding
- interview-only visuals
- white-collar corporate photography that weakens the high-volume thesis

#### Treatment rules

Images should feel:

- real
- calm but pressured
- slightly cinematic
- structured, not glossy
- integrated into the system, not decorative
- more documentary than campaign
- more observed than staged
- slightly early-2000s editorial rather than contemporary SaaS polish

Preferred treatment:

- crop tightly
- use repetition and geometry
- allow slight blur or motion where useful
- keep color subdued under the core palette
- let overlays feel functional, not futuristic
- keep the scene grounded enough that it still feels like hiring operations rather than infrastructure software
- prefer real photography language over design-language abstraction

Overlays can include:

- routing lines
- queue cues
- checkpoints
- labels
- state markers

Avoid:

- holograms
- sci-fi glow
- decorative particles
- floating glass effects

#### Hero ratio

- 60% system / typography / workflow panel
- 40% operational imagery or texture

#### Image system test

If the page looks like mission-critical infrastructure software with no obvious connection to hiring environments, the image system is failing.

If the page looks like logistics photography with a little UI on top, the image system is also failing.

The correct outcome is:

- serious AI software
- grounded in real operational hiring
- visibly moving work forward

### Prompting reference

For generated imagery, prefer prompts that describe:

- one real operational scene only
- documentary-style editorial photography
- candid workers secondary to the environment
- repetition, depth, movement, wear, and texture
- no numbers, no labels, no overlays, and no fake UI inside the image

Preferred scene types:

- warehouse aisles
- loading docks and staging areas
- kitchen or service lines
- repetitive production environments

Preferred photographic language:

- `Kodak Portra 400` with some `CineStill 400D` character
- `Leica M6` or `Contax G2`
- `35mm lens`
- fine film grain
- subtle halation
- slightly deeper blacks
- natural light
- realistic color
- slight motion blur where natural

Avoid in prompts:

- Breathe / product / category language
- system labels
- numbers
- dashboards
- floating interfaces
- holograms
- command-center aesthetics
- staged corporate stock-photo energy

Reference prompt pattern:

`Photorealistic documentary-style editorial photograph of a real high-volume frontline work environment. Show one specific operational scene with repetition, depth, tactile surfaces, believable imperfections, and candid human presence secondary to the environment. Early-2000s editorial photo feeling. Shot on Kodak Portra 400 with some CineStill 400D character, Leica M6 or Contax G2, 35mm lens. Fine film grain, subtle halation, slightly deeper blacks, natural light, realistic color, gentle motion blur where appropriate. No text overlays, no numbers, no labels added by the model, no dashboards, no floating UI, no holograms, no laptops, no command-center aesthetics, no staged corporate stock photo. Landscape composition for a landing page hero, tightly composed, highly realistic.`

### Reusable visual cues

- queue labels
- state pills
- throughput counters
- routing lines
- checkpoint markers
- recruiter review states

## Homepage Foundation

### Hero

Eyebrow:

`CANDIDATE OPERATIONS FOR HIGH-VOLUME TEAMS`

Headline:

`Breathe runs candidate operations for high-volume teams.`

Supporting copy:

`From application to ready-to-start, Breathe automates screening, outreach, interviews, documentation, and enrollment on top of your ATS and existing stack, so teams can fill more roles without adding recruiter load.`

Primary CTA:

`Book a demo`

Secondary CTA:

`See how it works`

Hero system panel should:

- show hiring-stage progression, not infra metrics
- show stages like intake, screen, outreach, interview, review, schedule, docs, ready-to-start
- use hiring labels such as role, site, shift, ATS sync, docs pending, review needed, next step
- avoid `TPS`, `P95`, region names, or infra-console language

### Proof bar

Primary proof line:

`From the team that increased recruiter productivity 7x at Job&Talent.`

Optional supporting proof slots:

- `Built for real high-volume hiring operations`
- `Works on top of the ATS teams already use`
- `From application to ready-to-start`
- future customer logos and metrics

Visual treatment:

- compact horizontal strip
- quiet and credible
- more proof ledger than logo carousel

### Friction

Headline:

`Volume breaks candidate operations first.`

Supporting copy:

`When every stage depends on recruiters chasing responses, scheduling interviews, moving candidates forward, collecting documentation, and coordinating handoffs across tools, throughput slows down long before demand does.`

Supporting points:

- `Too many applicants to process manually`
- `Too many handoffs across disconnected tools`
- `Too much recruiter time spent pushing the workflow forward`
- `Too much inconsistency between candidates, sites, and shifts`

Outcome framing line:

`Open roles stay open, candidates drop, and operations feel it.`

### Mechanism

Headline:

`Candidate operations, from application to ready-to-start.`

Supporting copy:

`Breathe uses AI to run screening, outreach, interviews, scheduling, documentation, enrollment, and recruiter review, so the process keeps moving without relying on recruiters to push every step by hand.`

Mechanism modules:

- `Screen`
  `Parse applications, apply rules, and move qualified candidates forward.`
- `Reach out`
  `Handle follow-up, reminders, and next-step communication automatically.`
- `Interview`
  `Run structured interviews and capture signals in a consistent format.`
- `Coordinate`
  `Move candidates across stages, trigger next actions, and keep the process aligned.`
- `Document`
  `Collect required information and keep documentation moving without manual chasing.`
- `Enroll`
  `Support handoff into enrollment and ready-to-start steps once a candidate is selected.`
- `Escalate`
  `Ask recruiters for input only where judgment or approval is needed.`

Bottom line:

`Candidate operations keep moving. Recruiters stay focused on decisions.`
